The Streaming Illusion

While streaming platforms tout accessibility and global reach, the economic reality for artists is grim. For every dollar paid by subscribers, artists typically receive just pennies after platforms, labels, and distributors take their cut.

A million streams might sound impressive, but it often translates to less than minimum wage compensation for the creators who made the music possible.

The Rights Trap

Beyond revenue shares, the industry's greatest exploitation comes through rights ownership. Most label contracts require artists to surrender ownership of their master recordings permanently.

This means even if an artist becomes wildly successful, they'll never fully own or control their greatest works - creating generational wealth for corporations instead of creators and their families.

THE 360 DEAL TRAP

The 360 deal has become the music industry's most controversial contractual arrangement, designed to maximize label profits while limiting artist autonomy.

HOW 360 DEALS WORK

Unlike traditional record deals that focused primarily on music sales, 360 deals entitle labels to percentages of virtually all artist revenue streams:

  • 50-85% of all music sales and streaming
  • 30-50% of merchandise revenue
  • 15-30% of touring income
  • 20-35% of brand deals and endorsements

Revenue Extraction Example

For an artist generating $100,000 across income streams:

Streaming ($40,000) Label takes: $20,000 (50%)

Merchandise ($30,000) Label takes: $9,000 (30%)

Touring ($20,000) Label takes: $3,000 (15%)

Brand Deals ($10,000) Label takes: $2,000 (20%)

TOTALS: $100,000

LABEL SHARE: $34,000 (34%)

Note: This doesn't include recoupment deductions from the artist's $66,000 remainder.

STREAMING ECONOMICS

The economics behind streaming services have created a system where most artists struggle to earn living wages from their music.

Spotify's Payment Model

Spotify uses a "stream share" payment system that pays rights holders (typically record labels or publishers), not artists directly.

Artists earn approximately one-third of a cent per stream, meaning an artist needs around 800,000 monthly streams just to earn minimum wage.

Meanwhile, Spotify reported a record 1.14 billion Euros in net income in 2024 and claims to have paid $10 billion to the music industry that same year - primarily to labels and publishers, not artists.

The Recoupment Racket

Recoupment is one of the most insidious aspects of traditional record deals - a high-interest loan system that keeps many artists in perpetual debt to their labels.

When labels cover expenses like studio time, video production, and marketing, these are not gifts but loans that must be repaid before an artist sees any royalty payments.

The critical catch: These expenses are recouped only from the artist's small portion of royalties, while the label continues receiving their much larger percentage unaffected.

In practical terms, an artist with a 15% royalty rate may have to generate millions in revenue before seeing their first royalty check beyond their initial advance.
